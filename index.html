Until Sunday morning, the Santacruz neighbourhood in Mumbai had received a staggering 427mm of rain in 24 hours. It was 133mm in the previous 24 hours. July is the city's wettest month with an average rainfall of 819mm. The first five days of the new month has already seen 675mm of rain. However, the India Meteorological Department said more heavy rain is likely across Mumbai and the surrounding areas for much of the week ahead. Warnings have also been issued advising people to stay away from the coast. Annual rainfall is essential in India as rains support two-thirds of the 1.3 billion population living in rural areas who rely on farming.
